Major Airports in India

Indira Gandhi International Airport (DEL), New Delhi

The busiest airport in India, DEL serves as the major international hub in the country. Known for its world-class facilities and Terminal 3, it's a key gateway to Northern India.

Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj International Airport (BOM), Mumbai

Located in Mumbai, BOM is India's second busiest airport and is well known for its stunning architecture, especially the award-winning Terminal 2.

Kempegowda International Airport (BLR), Bengaluru

BLR is a fast-growing airport in South India, serving the tech capital Bengaluru with modern terminals and eco-friendly operations.

Rajiv Gandhi International Airport (HYD), Hyderabad

HYD is known for its efficiency, excellent passenger experience, and recognition as one of the best airports in its category globally.

Chennai International Airport (MAA), Chennai

MAA serves as the major aviation hub for Tamil Nadu and South India, offering both domestic and international services since 1954.

Netaji Subhas Chandra Bose International Airport (CCU), Kolkata

CCU is the primary airport of Eastern India, named after the freedom fighter Netaji Subhas Chandra Bose, and is known for its large passenger terminal.

Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel International Airport (AMD), Ahmedabad

Serving Gujarat, AMD connects Western India to key international destinations and is named after India's Iron Man, Sardar Patel.

Cochin International Airport (COK), Kochi

COK is the world’s first fully solar-powered airport and a major gateway to Kerala, famous for its sustainability and modern design.

Pune International Airport (PNQ), Pune

PNQ serves the growing IT and industrial city of Pune and operates both domestic and limited international flights.

Goa International Airport (GOI), Goa

Located near Vasco da Gama, GOI caters to the tourism-heavy region of Goa, offering connections to major Indian cities and select international destinations.
